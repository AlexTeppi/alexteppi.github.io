An Overview of Ethiopia’s Forests

Ethiopia boasts a rich tapestry of forests that cover approximately 15% of the country’s land area. These forests range from tropical rainforests to montane forests and dry woodlands, each contributing uniquely to the nation’s ecological health...

Key Forest Zones in Southwest Ethiopia

  • Jimma Zone: Known for its montane forests and wild coffee cultivation.
  • Bonga Forest: Biodiversity hotspot in Kafa Zone, home to wild Coffea arabica.
  • Sheka Zone: A biosphere reserve supporting endangered species.
  • Gambela Region: Rich in wetlands and migratory birds with some forest areas.

The Socio-Economic Importance of Forests

These forests provide resources such as honey, medicinal plants, and wild fruits while helping regulate water, carbon, and pollination cycles. They also support traditional livelihoods and are part of Ethiopia’s sustainable development efforts.
